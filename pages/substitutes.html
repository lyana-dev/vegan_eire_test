Grain expectations - wholesome starts here.

Bulgur

The easy ancient grain

Fluffy, versatile and packed with nutrients - the easy grain that works with everything. No soaking, no fuss.

Amaranth

Amaranth :
Small grain, Big energy

Amaranth is a small, nutrient-dense grain with a mild and nutty flavor. It works well in both sweet and savory dishes, without overpowering other ingredients. It's a straightforward, versatile and genuinely satisfying.

Buckwheat

The gluten-free classic

Buckwheat has a bold, earthy character that gives dishes a grounded and satisfying feel. Its natural richness and hearty texture make it perfect for everything from cozy porridges to crisp salads and rustic bakes. It's the grain that brings a little soul to every bowl.

Barley

The comfort grain

Barley brings an easy kind of warmth to the table. Its gentle chew and subtle, nutty flavor make every dish feel instantly more comforting. Whether stirred into soups, tossed into salads or used as a base for cozy bowls, it adds heartiness without heaviness. Barley feels like a familiar friend - steady, nourishing and always welcoming.

Tastebud trouble makers.

Liquid Smoke

Smoke...without the fire

Liquid smoke brings the campfire without the camping. A tiny splash adds that slow-smoked depth that makes dishes feel bigger, bolder and a little more dramatic. It turns simple ingredients into something that tastes like they spent hours over glowing embers - even if they didn't get near a grill.

Nutritional Yeast

The golden touch

Nutritional yeast is the quiet hero of plant-based cooking - a golden sprinkle that somehow makes everything taste cheesier and more comforting. It brings gentle umami and a soft, savoury richness that slides into sauces, soups, pastas and popcorn...like it was always meant to be there.

Miso

The Umami of all umamis

Miso paste is umami in its coziest form - smooth, salty and full of gentle depth. Stirring it into a dish feels like switching on a warm light: everything suddenly becomes richer, rounder and more balanced. It's the ingredient that quietly ties a recipe together without ever stealing the spotlight.

Kala Namak

A pinch with personality

Black salt has an unmistakable aroma and a flavour that feels both ancient and a little mischievous. Traditionally used across South Asia, it brings a distinct sulphur and exotic allure to it. It's the kind of ingredient that makes you feel like you're tapping into a deeper culinary tradition.
